Dandelions

Source

This is a dandelion flower (Taraxacum officinale). These are the most common type of dandelion you will see around.
As you can see, a flowering dandelion will most commonly have a bright yellow flower sitting atop it's stem.

Source

This is a dandelion seedhead. After a certain amount of time, the yellow dandelions will change into these fluffier looking plants.
The fluff, in fact, are actually seeds. These seeds will eventually be blown away by wind or eaten by animals in order to disperse and make more dandelions somewhere else.

Milkweed

Source

Milkweed (Asclepias lanceolata) is a plant most commonly grown for butterflies. Butterflies (mostly monarch butterflies) are very fond of this particular plant.
They commonly lay eggs in the leaves and use the bright red flowers on top for nectar. There is, however, a certain species of milkweed called Tropical Milkweed that can affect a monarch butterfly's migration pattern, so it is not reccommended to use that type of milkweed.
